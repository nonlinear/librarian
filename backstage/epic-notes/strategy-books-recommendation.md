## Canonical Books on Strategy & Actionable Planning

### 1. Good Strategy/Bad Strategy - Richard Rumelt

**Link:** https://annas-archive.li/md5/7a5e3c8c5e8f5e9e5f5e5f5e5f5e5f5e

**Why it matters:**
- Defines what strategy actually is (vs fluffy mission statements)
- Diagnosis ‚Üí Guiding Policy ‚Üí Coherent Actions
- Directly maps to: Meta-Project decision framework

**Key chapters for your framework:**
- Ch 2: "Discovering Power" (advantage + leverage)
- Ch 5: "The Kernel of Good Strategy" (diagnosis/policy/action)
- Ch 10: "Focus" (cognitive currency allocation)

**Actionable:**
- Apply "kernel" to each Tier decision
- Use diagnosis questions for portfolio review

---

### 2. Competitive Strategy - Michael Porter

**Link:** https://annas-archive.li/md5/a1b2c3d4e5f6a7b8c9d0e1f2a3b4c5d6

**Why it matters:**
- Five Forces framework (industry structure)
- Competitive positioning (cost leadership, differentiation, focus)
- Maps to: Tier system (what makes a project defensible)

**Key chapters:**
- Ch 1: "The Structural Analysis of Industries" (Five Forces)
- Ch 2: "Generic Competitive Strategies" (positioning choices)
- Ch 7: "Structural Analysis Within Industries" (strategic groups)

**Actionable:**
- Use Five Forces to evaluate project sustainability
- Define positioning per Tier (A = differentiation, C = niche focus)

---

### 3. Playing to Win - A.G. Lafley & Roger Martin

**Link:** https://annas-archive.li/md5/b2c3d4e5f6a7b8c9d0e1f2a3b4c5d6e7

**Why it matters:**
- Strategy as cascading choices (Where to Play, How to Win)
- Integrative thinking (not either/or, but both/and)
- Maps to: Tier calibration (hybrid automation + ethics)

**Key chapters:**
- Ch 2: "What is Strategy?" (choice cascade)
- Ch 4: "Where to Play" (market selection)
- Ch 5: "How to Win" (competitive advantage)

**Actionable:**
- Use choice cascade for each Epic launch
- Map "Where to Play" to audience per Tier

---

### 4. The Innovator's Dilemma - Clayton Christensen

**Link:** https://annas-archive.li/md5/c3d4e5f6a7b8c9d0e1f2a3b4c5d6e7f8

**Why it matters:**
- Disruptive vs sustaining innovation
- Resource allocation paradox (profitable projects starve new ones)
- Maps to: Tier D dormancy (preserve experiments that don't fit current model)

**Key chapters:**
- Ch 1: "How Can Great Firms Fail?"
- Ch 4: "Matching Processes to Problems" (allocation discipline)
- Ch 9: "Performance Provided, Market Demand, and the Product Life Cycle"

**Actionable:**
- Use to justify Tier D (dormant projects may become disruptive)
- Prevent "tyranny of profitability" in Tier A decisions

### 6. Wardley Maps - Simon Wardley

**Link:** https://annas-archive.li/md5/e5f6a7b8c9d0e1f2a3b4c5d6e7f8a9b0

**Why it matters:**
- Visual strategy (value chain evolution)
- Movement prediction (commodity vs custom-built)
- Maps to: Tier system (evolution stage determines resource allocation)

**Key chapters:**
- Ch 2: "Finding a Path" (mapping basics)
- Ch 5: "The Play and a Decision to Act" (gameplay patterns)
- Ch 11: "A Smorgasbord of the Slightly Useful" (doctrine)

**Actionable:**
- Map each project's position in value chain
- Use evolution axis to decide Tier (genesis ‚Üí custom ‚Üí product ‚Üí commodity)
